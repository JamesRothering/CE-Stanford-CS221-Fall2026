## Key Learnings

1. **Dartmouth Workshop (1956)**: John McCarthy (Stanford AI Lab founder), Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon; simulate all learning/intelligence.[file:18]

2. **John McCarthy**: Dartmouth organizer; symbolic/logical AI tradition.[file:18]

3. **Marvin Minsky**: Dartmouth; *Perceptrons* (1969, w/ Papert) showed linear classifiers fail XOR; shifted to symbolic AI.[file:18]

4. **Claude Shannon**: Dartmouth; information theory.[file:18]

5. **First AI Winter**: Translation flop ("spirit willing/flesh weak" → "vodka good/meat rotten"); compute shortage, exponential search, no language nuance.[file:18]

6. **Early AI Contributions**: Lisp (GC, high-level langs), time-sharing, modeling/inference split.[file:18]

7. **Expert Systems (70s-80s)**: Rule-based knowledge for narrow tasks (diagnosis, orders); industrial success; failed on brittleness.[file:18]

8. **Second AI Winter**: Rules insufficient; over-promising.[file:18]

9. **McCulloch & Pitts (1943)**: Neurons as logic; neural net roots.[file:18]

10. **Backpropagation (80s)**: Train multilayer NNs.[file:18]

11. **Yann LeCun (1989)**: CNNs for digits; USPS ZIP codes.[file:18]

12. **AlexNet (2012)**: ImageNet revolution.[file:18]
